# Chinese Grilled Chicken Skewers

Not super authentic, but very delicious. I subbed out the chinese cooking wine and sugar for mirin, as it's basically cooking wine + sugar. This is best cooked on a low and slow grill. 

## Ingredients:

*Skewers*

- 5 lb chicken thighs, boneless, skinless

*Marinade*

- 6 tbsp oyster sauce
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp Mirin
- 2 tsp sambal or sriracha 
- 2 tsp sesame oil (optional)
- 6 cloves garlic, crushed
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated


## Directions

1. In a small bowl, add all marinade ingredients. Stir to combine.
2. Cut chicken thighs in half, length-wise. 
3. Toss chicken and marinade together in large bowl. Marinate at least 1 hour, or overnight in the regrigerator.
4. Add chicken to skewers, bunching them up a bit. Reserve marinade and transfer to small vessel.
5. Heat propane grill on low to 300°F or so. If using charcoal, use a small amount of coal or use indirect heat. The key is cooking this over low, dry heat.
6. Add chicken to grill.
7. Every 3 minutes, rotate 45 degrees on the axis of the skewer. Baste with remaining marinade. Repeat until browned and dry on the outside, and fully cooked but still moiste on the inside.
8. Remove from heat, drizzle with sesame oil, and serve.
